Abstract

Mobile manipulation policies require demonstrations that span a wide distribution of base-induced viewpoints with coupled base-arm action, making data scaling the dominant bottleneck of the setting. Existing demonstration augmentation methods either stay restricted to viewpoint-invariant edits or require inputs that ordinary VLA training data lacks, such as calibrated 3D capture, simulators, or object-pose tracking. We propose NoDA, or Novel Demonstration Augmentation, a calibration-free framework that consumes a standard VLA tuple of multi-camera RGB and end-effector trajectory and emits new demonstrations in the same format. NoDA splits each demonstration at the moment of first contact: in the approach phase the base is freely perturbed and the new viewpoint is rendered by a multi-view 3D foundation model; in the contact phase the base is perturbed only slightly while the end-effector world trajectory is held identical to the source, preserving the object's world trajectory across augmentations. The foundation model is fine-tuned once on AgiBot World Beta as NoDA-FT and deployed zero-shot to user-collected data, with no per-setup calibration or retraining. Real-robot experiments on π0.5 achieve up to a 1.35× data-efficiency gain across six mobile manipulation tasks.

Method Overview

A demonstration decomposes naturally into an approach phase, in which the gripper moves toward the object but has not yet made contact, and a contact-manipulation phase, in which the gripper is in contact and base-arm coordination drives the task forward. In the approach phase NoDA freely perturbs the base trajectory and renders the resulting novel viewpoints with a multi-view 3D foundation model that produces metric-scaled camera poses and per-pixel Gaussian primitives from RGB alone. In the contact phase NoDA perturbs the base while holding the end-effector world trajectory identical to the source, so the object world trajectory is unchanged and the augmented end-effector-relative-to-base pose follows directly from the perturbed base — without joint angles or kinematic-chain inversion. The foundation model is fine-tuned once on AgiBot World Beta as NoDA-FT and deployed zero-shot to user-collected data, with no per-setup calibration, scanning, or retraining.

Tasks & Scenes

Six real-robot mobile manipulation tasks spanning the base-arm coordination spectrum, from pure-base navigation to bimanual transport: open microwave, open drawer, pick and place corn, push chair, open fridge, and carry box.

Results

Real-robot success rates (out of 30 trials) across six tasks and four source-data budgets, with and without NoDA. The NoDA condition adds N = 4 augmented episodes per source (a 5× training set). NoDA improves over the raw baseline in every cell, with mean absolute gain stable at 8.3%–9.4% across all budgets — up to a 1.35× data-efficiency gain at low source budgets.
